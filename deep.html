What is Deep Learning?

Deep Learning is a part of Machine Learning — it is used when tasks are too complicated for basic ML. Deep Learning uses something called neural networks, which are inspired by the human brain.

These networks have many layers, like a sandwich or onion — data goes through many layers to become smarter. That is why it is called “deep” learning — it goes deep into the data.

Real-life Use of Deep Learning • Self-Driving Cars: Cars use deep learning to detect traffic signs, pedestrians, and obstacles.
• Face Recognition: Deep learning is used to match faces, even with changes in light or expressions.
• Voice Assistants: Deep learning helps understand different accents and languages.
• Medical Diagnosis: Machines can help doctors by identifying signs of diseases from X-rays or scans.

Deep learning needs a lot of data and strong computers, but the results are amazing — sometimes better than humans in specific tasks!
